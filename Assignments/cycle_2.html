In Fusion 360, I started by sketching the needle holder with the mirror tool and extruded it to be about 5mm long. I copied this to create 10 needle holders in 2 rows of 5's.

The rest of the box was made by sketching rectangles, joining them together and shelling them to create a hollow box.

The lid was created similarly, although I modified the rectangles by removing two sides so that it can slide onto the box.

In PrusaSlicer, I ran into an issue with the wall dimensions when splicing and had to increase the thickness to about 1mm. After setting it up for the Prusa Mini and setting the layer height to 0.2mm, I was able to print it in about 30 minutes.

After cleaning the plate with alcohol, I was able to load my design into the 3-D printer and begin printing.

My first attempt printed the lid perfectly, but the base of my box was too thin. I had to go back into Fusion 360 and extrude the bottom again by 1mm.

After another 30 minutes or so, my box was reprinted.

The lid fits onto the box and is able to slide on and off.
