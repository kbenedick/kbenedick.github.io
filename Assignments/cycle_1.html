Canvas

The photo I chose for my puzzle is a picture I took of my dad's grave earlier this spring for the anniversary of his death. My sister and I would pick flowers to create makeshift bouquets that we would leave for him.

First, I converted the photo to black and white, and then I traced the image.

However, since we haven't gotten a chance to clean the physical grave, I touched it up with the paintbrush tool.

I made a rectangle that fit around the grave and then drew out puzzle pieces with my wacom tablet to fill it. However, I can't make perfect lines that connect to each other, so I had to go back in to individually check each line and ensure that the points connect together.

And lastly, I rasterized the grave and made sure the puzzle piece lines were all made with a brush stroke of 0.01mm so that the machine would know to cut through rather than engrave.

Once the laser cutting machine was working again, I did the engraving first after calibrating the machine and using a lower power laser. This process took about 10 minutes.

After engraving it, I cut out the puzzle pieces and had to do this process twice in order to fully cut through.

And finally, I put all the pieces together, which was a somewhat difficult process since there were gaps between the connecting pieces.
